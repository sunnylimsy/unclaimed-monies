How to Make a Claim — 3 Steps

1
Search for your name or address

Enter your name (or the name of a deceased person you are claiming on behalf of) and/or your last known address. Partial searches work — e.g. searching "Tan" will return all names containing "Tan".

2
Click "Make a Claim" on the matching record

A window will open with a pre-filled email and instructions. Copy the email content or click "Open in Email" to launch your email app with details filled in. The subject line should be: "Please process my claim".

3
Send your email with supporting documents

Attach the relevant documents (see below) and send to the contact officer at the relevant agency. The agency will verify your identity and process your claim.

Documents to Prepare

The exact documents required vary by agency. The following is a general guide — always confirm with the agency when you contact them.

For all claims (bring these regardless)

  • Your NRIC (Singapore Citizens / PRs) or Passport (foreigners)
  • Proof of the address listed in the record (e.g. old utility bill, bank statement, SingPass records)

If you are claiming for yourself

  • NRIC / Passport matching the name in the record
  • Any documents related to the original transaction (e.g. old tax notice, levy receipt, court document)

If you are claiming on behalf of a deceased person

  • Death Certificate of the deceased
  • Grant of Probate or Letters of Administration (if the estate has been administered)
  • Your own NRIC as the executor or administrator
  • If no Grant of Probate: contact the Public Trustee's Office — they can assist with small estates

If you are claiming on behalf of a company

  • ACRA Business Profile of the company
  • Authorisation letter from the company (signed by a director)
  • Your own NRIC

Common categories and what to expect

  • Tax Refunds Contact IRAS — bring NRIC and your tax reference number
  • Levy Bond / Foreign Worker Levy Contact MOM Work Pass Division — bring company UEN and employment records
  • Bailiff's Account / Court Deposits Contact the Supreme Court or State Courts — may require a lawyer
  • Tender Deposits Contact the relevant agency — bring original tender documents
  • Unredeemed Pledges Contact Registry of Pawnbrokers under MinLaw
  • Deceased Estate / CPF Monies Contact Public Trustee's Office — bring death certificate and proof of relationship

Frequently Asked Questions

Does appearing in the register mean I will definitely get the money?

No. The register only shows that money is being held in your name. You must prove you are the rightful owner by presenting the correct documents to the agency.

Is there a time limit to claim?

There is no fixed deadline stated on the register, but it is advisable to claim as soon as possible. For Supreme Court funds, unclaimed amounts may be transferred to the government after 6 years.

Do I need a lawyer?

For most straightforward claims (tax refunds, levy deposits), you do not need a lawyer. For court-related deposits or complex estate matters, legal advice is recommended.

Is this site affiliated with the Singapore government?

No. This is an independent search tool. All data is sourced from www.unclaimedmonies.gov.sg, the official government registry. Always verify information directly with the relevant agency.
